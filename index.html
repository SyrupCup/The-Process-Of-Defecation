The Process Of Defecation

Main Topic:

The process of defecation, or bowel movement, involves the elimination of waste material from the digestive system through the anus.

The process begins when the rectum, which is the final portion of the large intestine, fills with stool. This triggers a sensation of needing to defecate. The external anal sphincter, a ring-shaped muscle that encircles the anus, must then relax to allow the stool to pass out of the body.

The act of defecation can be facilitated by the abdominal muscles, which contract to increase intra-abdominal pressure and push the stool out.

As for sources, the process of defecation is a well-documented physiological process in human anatomy and physiology
